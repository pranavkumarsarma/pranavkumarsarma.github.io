02 · AI Quality

Testing AI that doesn't behave.

Traditional QA checks that button X does action Y. AI agents change their output every run. Testing them means building evaluation infrastructure, not checklists: golden datasets, LLM-as-judge scoring, and observability that catches quality drift before it ships.

01

Golden datasets

Curate real production failures and edge cases into a frozen evaluation set.

02

Multi-turn & adversarial

Push agents through multi-turn flows and deliberate edge-case prompts.

03

LLM-as-judge

Score non-deterministic output on faithfulness, grounding, and tool use.

04

Observability

Trace every run in Langfuse and Oodle; watch for quality drift over time.

05

Regression gates

Block prompt changes that quietly regress quality before they ship.

05 · Tools & Frameworks

Learning by doing.

Every framework I've worked with came from building something real with it, not tutorials.

Browser automation: Puppeteer, Playwright, WebdriverIO

Tested all three through production POCs. Playwright won for speed and reliability; I use WebdriverIO for its architecture and CI/CD integration. Every suite is wired to post results to Slack channels for real-time team visibility.

AI-powered test generation: BrowserUse + WebUI, PlaywrightMCP

Built POCs where English-language test descriptions become automated scripts. Tested both frameworks; they excel for rapid prototyping and regression suites on familiar flows.

The POC approach

I don't wait to be an expert before shipping. I build a small POC with every tool worth considering, measure it against my current stack, and iterate. This is why I can move fast between frameworks and pick the right one for the job.
